## Performance Review

> ~571 tokens

### Feedback Synthesis Workflow

1. Collect input: peer feedback, self-assessment, manager observations
2. Identify consistent themes (mentioned by 2+ sources)
3. Separate strengths, growth areas, and mixed/divergent feedback
4. Check alignment between self-assessment and peer feedback
5. Draft review with specific behavioral examples
6. Set SMART development goals

### Feedback Synthesis Format

```
Feedback Synthesis -- <name> (<period>)

Sources: <N> peer reviews + self-assessment

Consistent Strengths (mentioned by 3+ peers):
1. <strength> -- <supporting quote or evidence>

Growth Areas (mentioned by 2+ peers):
1. <area> -- <supporting quote or evidence>

Mixed Feedback (divergent views):
- <topic>: <differing perspectives> -- worth discussing

Self-Assessment Alignment:
<where self-assessment matches or diverges from peer feedback>
```

### Performance Review Document Template

```
Performance Review -- <name> | <period>

Overall Assessment: <Exceeds / Meets / Below Expectations>

<1-2 sentence summary of the period>

Key Accomplishments:
- <specific achievement with measurable impact>

Strengths:
<strength title>: <behavioral evidence and impact>

Areas for Development:
<area title>: <specific observation, why it matters, forward-looking framing>

Goals for Next Period:
1. <SMART goal: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound>
2. <SMART goal>
3. <SMART goal>

Manager Notes:
(Space for personal observations and context)
```

### SMART Goal Examples

| Goal | S | M | A | R | T |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|
| Present 2 technical proposals to non-engineering stakeholders using business-impact-first structure | Specific audience + format | 2 presentations | Achievable for senior IC | Addresses communication gap | Next quarter |
| Delegate one project workstream fully to a junior engineer | Specific scope | 1 workstream | Achievable with coaching | Addresses delegation growth area | Next quarter |

### Rules

- Never attribute specific feedback to named individuals in the summary
- Balance positive and constructive feedback (never all negative or all positive)
- Use specific behavioral examples, not vague generalizations
- Frame growth areas as forward-looking development opportunities
- Always include actionable, measurable goals
- Highlight where self-assessment diverges from peer feedback (discussion opportunity)
