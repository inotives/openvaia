## Literature Review

> ~655 tokens

### Search Strategy
When researching a topic:
1. **Define scope** — narrow the research question to something specific and answerable
2. **Identify keywords** — primary terms + synonyms + related concepts
3. **Find seminal works** — highly cited foundational papers in the field
4. **Trace forward** — recent papers citing the seminal works
5. **Identify gaps** — what has not been studied, what is contradictory

### Paper Summary Template
```
**[Authors] ([Year]).** "[Title]." *[Journal]*, [Volume]([Issue]), [Pages].
- **Key Finding:** [one sentence]
- **Method:** [study type, sample size, approach]
- **Limitations:** [noted weaknesses or biases]
- **Cited by:** [count] — [significance note]
- **Relevance:** [why this matters to the review topic]
```

### Literature Review Structure Options

**By theme:**
- Group papers by subtopic or finding
- Best for: broad topics with distinct sub-areas

**By methodology:**
- Group by research approach (qualitative, quantitative, mixed)
- Best for: methods-focused reviews

**By chronology:**
- Trace how understanding evolved over time
- Best for: showing development of a field

**By argument:**
- Group into supporting vs contradicting evidence
- Best for: evaluating a specific claim

### Literature Review Outline Template
```
## Literature Review: [Topic]

### Introduction
- Research question: [specific question]
- Scope: [time period, field, inclusion criteria]

### [Theme 1]
- [Paper summaries organized by theme]
- Synthesis: [what the evidence collectively shows]

### [Theme 2]
- [Paper summaries]
- Synthesis: [collective findings]

### Gaps and Contradictions
- [What has not been studied]
- [Where findings conflict and possible explanations]

### Conclusion
- [Summary of current state of knowledge]
- [Suggested directions for future research]
```

### Citation Rules
- Always provide: authors, year, title, journal/source
- Distinguish clearly between your summary and direct quotes
- Note limitations and potential biases of every cited study
- Never fabricate sources — state explicitly when uncertain or unable to verify
- Flag when a claim relies on a single study vs broad consensus

### Citation Formats Quick Reference

**APA 7th:** Author, A. B. (Year). Title. *Journal*, *Vol*(Issue), Pages. https://doi.org/xxx
**Chicago:** Author. "Title." *Journal* Vol, no. Issue (Year): Pages.
**BibTeX:**
```
@article{key,
  author={Last, First},
  title={Title},
  journal={Journal},
  volume={Vol},
  year={Year},
  pages={Pages}
}
```
