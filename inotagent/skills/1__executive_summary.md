## Executive Summary

> ~1200 tokens

### SCQA Framework (McKinsey)

Structure any business communication as:
1. **Situation**: What is the current state? (context everyone agrees on)
2. **Complication**: What changed or went wrong? (the tension)
3. **Question**: What do we need to decide? (implied or explicit)
4. **Answer**: What should we do? (your recommendation, stated up front)

Lead with the Answer when the audience is senior. Lead with Situation when the audience needs context first.

### Pyramid Principle (BCG)

- **Top-down**: State the conclusion first, then support with evidence
- **MECE grouping**: Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive (no overlaps, no gaps)
- **Rule of 3-5**: Group findings into 3-5 key points (cognitive limit)
- **So-what test**: Every finding must answer "so what does this mean for the business?"

### Executive Summary Template

Target: 325-475 words (readable in < 3 minutes)

```
## 1. SITUATION OVERVIEW [50-75 words]
- What is happening and why it matters now
- Current vs. desired state gap

## 2. KEY FINDINGS [125-175 words]
- 3-5 most critical insights
- Each with >= 1 quantified or comparative data point
- **Bold the strategic implication in each**
- Order by business impact (highest first)

## 3. BUSINESS IMPACT [50-75 words]
- Quantify potential gain/loss (revenue, cost, market share)
- Risk or opportunity magnitude (% or probability)
- Time horizon for realization

## 4. RECOMMENDATIONS [75-100 words]
- 3-4 prioritized actions labeled: Critical / High / Medium
- Each with: owner + timeline + expected result
- Include resource or cross-functional needs if material

## 5. NEXT STEPS [25-50 words]
- 2-3 immediate actions (within 30 days)
- Decision point + deadline
```

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Total length: 325-475 words (max 500)
- [ ] Every finding includes >= 1 quantified data point
- [ ] Strategic implications are bolded
- [ ] Content ordered by business impact
- [ ] Recommendations have: owner + timeline + expected result
- [ ] No assumptions beyond provided data
- [ ] Data gaps and uncertainties flagged explicitly
- [ ] Tone: decisive, factual, outcome-driven

### Recommendation Format (Bain)

Each recommendation follows:

```
**[Priority Level]**: [Specific Action]
Owner: [Role/Name] | Timeline: [Date] | Expected Result: [Quantified outcome]
```

Priority levels:
- **Critical**: Must act now; delay = material risk or missed opportunity
- **High**: Act within 30 days; significant impact on objectives
- **Medium**: Act within quarter; meaningful but not urgent improvement

### Writing Rules

1. **Quantify everything**: "Revenue dropped 34% QoQ, from $12M to $7.9M" not "revenue decreased significantly"
2. **Impact over description**: Focus on what it means, not what happened
3. **Active voice**: "CMO to launch campaign by June 15" not "a campaign should be launched"
4. **No filler**: Every sentence must either provide evidence or drive a decision
5. **Comparative framing**: Compare to previous period, competitor, benchmark, or target
6. **Flag unknowns**: "Data unavailable for Q4; estimate based on Q3 trends" -- never hide gaps

### Scenario Analysis Format

When uncertainty is high, present three cases:

| Scenario | Assumption | Revenue Impact | Probability |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Best case | [condition] | +$X (+Y%) | Z% |
| Base case | [condition] | +$X (+Y%) | Z% |
| Worst case | [condition] | -$X (-Y%) | Z% |

**Expected value**: weighted sum across scenarios.
